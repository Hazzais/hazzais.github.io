Work

I currently work as a data scientist in Cheltenham, United Kingdom for UCAS (the Universities and Colleges Admissions Service). My role has involved many different functions, including safely processing large amounts of sensitive data, leading and running services for external customers, supporting development for my business unit, and building statistical and predictive models.

I have also been involved in two published reports (linked below). The first was written entirely by myself, whilst for the second, my contribution was the statistical models in the annex section.

Education

I completed a four year Integrated Masters Physics Degree at the University of Nottingham in 2016 with first class honours. Whilst my degree covered a broad range of areas in physics, I mostly chose astrophysics-based modules. A third year project involved investigating Active Galactic Nuclei using data analysis on real data. My final dissertation involved investigating the potential for directly detecting dark matter by simulating experiments. This involved hypothesis testing and Monte Carlo methods.
